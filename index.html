The Facts

Did You Know?

Fast Fashion is a term we’ve been hearing a lot lately but what does it actually mean?
“Fast fashion can be defined as cheap, trendy clothing, that samples ideas from the catwalk or celebrity culture and turns them into garments in high street stores at breakneck speed.” – Good on You

In Ireland alone, half a tonne of clothing is dumped into landfill every minute. That amount produces over 12 tonnes of carbon emissions – the same as driving 65,000 kilometres in a car. Buying just one white cotton shirt produces the same amount of emissions as driving 56 kilometres in a car.

    Globally, the Fast Fashion Industry:
  • produces ~1 billion garments annually and emits 1.2 billion tons of CO2 equivalent per year
  • is second only to oil as the world’s largest polluter
  • is responsible for producing 20% of global wastewater

So What Can We Do?

As consumers there are plenty of changes, big and small, we can make to our shopping habits to make them more sustainable and reduce our impact on the environment. It’s not only about where we shop but also how we shop. Reducing the amount of new items we buy by shopping in vintage and charity shops or buying second hand either in store or online from sites like Ebay or Poshmark. Using sites like Again With Love to sell items you no longer want or use. Borrow it from sites like Rent the Runway for that statement piece you’re probably only going to wear once anyway! Mend, tailor, upcycle your old favourites. Go to swap shops or even get a group of your mates together and host your own! And if you do buy new, consider choosing ethical or eco brands. Check out the Fashion Transparency Index, it reviews and ranks 200 of the biggest global fashion and apparel brands and retailers according to how much information they disclose about their suppliers, supply chain policies and practices, and social and environmental impact.

Charity Shops

At a local level, charity shops are a great shout. Not only are you getting some seriously great pieces at ridiculously discounted prices, you’re also helping some amazing causes while you’re at it! And Dublin is full of them. From Rathmines down through Camden St, Aungier St and George’s Street and Capel St. on the Northside you’ll find Oxfam, Enable Ireland, St Vincent de Paul, Irish Cancer Society, Simon Community & Age Action - to name but a few!
(Click the links to find your local store.)
And of course it’s not just clothes, there’s shoes, handbags, accessories, jewellery, vinyl, books and all kinds of wonderful things. Now you can even bag some charity shop bargins from the comfort of your couch with Thriftify.
Don’t forget to return the favour and donate your unwanted clothes to your local charity shop!
